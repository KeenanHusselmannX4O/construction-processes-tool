1. Purpose

Register all potential variations from the moment they arise (design change, site condition, employer instruction) and conduct a thorough cost impact assessment before formal approval. This sub-process ensures no variation goes unrecorded, cost implications are assessed proactively, and the employer is informed before approving additional expenditure.

Trigger

PA instruction; design team revised drawing; contractor notice of site condition; employer change request

Standard

JBCC clauses 17-19 (Instructions & Adjustments); NEC clause 60-65 (Compensation Events)

Output

Variation register entry; cost impact assessment; employer approval request; approved VO for payment inclusion

3. Variation Valuation Methods

MethodWhen UsedDescription
Contract RatesSimilar work exists in BoQApply existing BoQ rates to varied quantities — preferred method
Analogous RatesSimilar but not identical work in BoQAdjust existing rates proportionally for different scope
Star RatesNo comparable BoQ itemBuild up new rate from first principles (labour + material + plant + OH&P)
Day WorksCannot predetermine scopeTime and materials at day work rates in BoQ
Lump Sum QuotationClearly defined additional scopeContractor provides fixed price; QS assesses reasonableness

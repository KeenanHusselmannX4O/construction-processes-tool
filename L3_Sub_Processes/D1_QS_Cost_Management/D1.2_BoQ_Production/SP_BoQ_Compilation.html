1. Purpose

Compile measured quantities into a structured Bill of Quantities document following ASAQS conventions. The BoQ becomes the pricing document for tenderers and the contractual basis for payment during construction.

3. BoQ Section Structure

SectionContentPurpose
PreliminariesGeneral and project-specific requirements, contractor obligationsNon-measurable costs: site establishment, management, insurance
PreamblesTrade-specific measurement rules and material specificationsDefine how items are measured and what materials satisfy descriptions
Measured WorksTrade sections with item descriptions, units, quantitiesPriced by tenderers — forms basis of contract sum
Provisional SumsAllowances for undefined or specialist workDefined PS (contractor profit only) and Undefined PS (full mark-up)
Prime Cost ItemsNominated supplier/subcontractor allowancesMaterials at specified quality with contractor attendance
Day WorksHourly / daily rates for unforeseen workUsed for work not measurable from drawings
SummaryCollection of section totals into contract sumSingle point for total tender price

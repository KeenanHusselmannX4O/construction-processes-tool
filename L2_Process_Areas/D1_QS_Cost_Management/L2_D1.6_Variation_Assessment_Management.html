1. Process Area Purpose

Variation Assessment & Management handles the cost assessment and financial impact of all changes to the contracted scope of work. Under JBCC, the Principal Agent issues variation orders (VOs), and the QS assesses the cost impact using contract rates, negotiated rates, or market-based pricing for new items. Variations are the primary driver of cost overruns — effective management here is critical to project financial control.

Standards: JBCC Series 2000 Clause 17 (Works Orders) · NEC4 Clause 60-66 (Compensation Events) · ASAQS Practice Notes on Variation Assessment

3. Inputs & Outputs

Inputs

  • PA site instructions / works orders
  • Revised drawings from architect/engineer
  • Contract BoQ rates for applicable items
  • Contractor quotations for new items
  • Market prices for star-rate items
  • Previous VO register

Outputs

  • Variation cost assessment report
  • Updated VO register (cumulative)
  • Approved variation value for payment inclusion
  • Budget impact notification (→ D1.7)
  • Input to monthly valuation (→ D1.5)
  • Input to final account (→ D1.8)

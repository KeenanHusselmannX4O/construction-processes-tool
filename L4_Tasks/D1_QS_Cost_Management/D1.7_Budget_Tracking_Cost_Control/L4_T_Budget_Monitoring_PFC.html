1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Budget Monitoring & Projected Final Cost (PFC) sub-process into granular work instructions. The PFC is the QS's primary financial control instrument — it predicts the total expected project cost at completion by combining committed costs, approved and anticipated variations, CPA projections, provisional sum adjustments, and remaining professional fees. These tasks cover the full monthly cycle: collecting expenditure data, updating the variation forecast, assessing PS exposure, projecting CPA to completion, calculating the PFC, preparing variance analysis, issuing early warnings, and presenting at the monthly project meeting. Per SACPCMP Practice Note 3, the PFC must be maintained monthly throughout the construction phase.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.7.1-01

Collect Latest Payment Certificate Data & Cumulative Expenditure

Obtain the latest payment certificate data from D1.5 and compile the cumulative expenditure to date. This forms the "actual" baseline against which the budget forecast is built.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
1 working day (after IPC issue)
Inputs
Latest D1.5 payment certificate; payment certificate register; contractor payment records
Outputs
Current expenditure summary; cumulative certified to date; payments made to date
Tools
Payment certificate register; financial tracking spreadsheet; platform finance module
Standard
SACPCMP Practice Note 3; SACQSP cost reporting guidelines
  • Work Instruction: Obtain the latest signed IPC from the D1.5 process — record: cert number, date, gross value, nett amount due
  • Update the payment certificate register with the latest certificate data
  • Compile cumulative expenditure: total certified to date (gross), total retention held, total paid to date, outstanding amounts
  • Reconcile against the employer's payment records to confirm all previous certificates have been paid
  • Flag any late payments — interest implications per JBCC clause 31 (interest on late payment)
  • ✓ Quality Check: Certificate register up to date; cumulative figures reconcile to individual certificates; payment status confirmed
TASK 1.7.1-02

Update Variation Register Status & Forecast

Review and update the D1.6 variation register, classifying each variation as Approved (agreed value), Pending (submitted but not agreed), or Anticipated (likely but not yet formally submitted). Assign monetary estimates to pending and anticipated items for PFC calculation.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
1 working day
Inputs
D1.6 variation register; PA instructions; pending quotations; risk register
Outputs
Updated variation forecast with approved, pending, and anticipated totals
Tools
Variation register; risk assessment template; PFC variation schedule
Standard
JBCC clauses 17–19; NEC4 clauses 60–65; SACQSP variation management practice
  • Work Instruction: Review each entry in the variation register — update the status based on latest information
  • For Approved VOs: confirm the agreed value is correct and capture any partially completed VO work progress
  • For Pending VOs: update the QS estimate based on latest information; note the expected approval timeline
  • For Anticipated VOs: assess the likelihood (low/medium/high) and estimate the cost range based on professional judgement
  • Compile three totals: (A) Approved VO total, (B) Pending VO total (best estimate), (C) Anticipated VO total (weighted by probability)
  • All three categories feed into the PFC at different confidence levels
  • ✓ Quality Check: Every register entry reviewed and updated; estimates documented with rationale; three-tier forecast compiled
TASK 1.7.1-03

Assess Remaining Provisional Sum Exposure

Review the D1.4-SP-003 Provisional Sum (PS) register and assess the remaining exposure. For expended PS items, record actual costs. For un-instructed PS items, estimate the likely expenditure based on design team input and project progress.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
D1.4-SP-003 PS register; design team input; nominated subcontractor quotes
Outputs
PS forecast: expended actual + estimate for unspent; PS savings or overruns identified
Tools
PS register; PS forecast template
Standard
JBCC PS clauses (Defined & Undefined); GCC 2015 clause 49
  • Work Instruction: Review each PS item in the register — classify as: Expended (actual cost known), Instructed (quotation received), or Not Yet Instructed
  • For Expended items: record the actual cost and calculate the saving or overrun vs the original PS allowance
  • For Instructed items: record the latest quotation or agreed value
  • For Not Yet Instructed items: consult the design team for likelihood of instruction and expected scope; estimate the cost
  • Sum all categories to produce the PS forecast total and compare against the original PS allowance in the BoQ
  • ✓ Quality Check: All PS items classified; expended items have actual costs; estimates reasonable and documented
TASK 1.7.1-04

Project CPA to Completion Using Latest Index Trends

Project the Contract Price Adjustment (CPA) to the expected completion date using the latest BER Building Cost Index data and Stats SA PPI trends. Extrapolate from the current index trajectory to estimate the CPA value at project completion.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
CPA to date from D1.4-SP-002; latest BER publication; Stats SA PPI data; expected completion date
Outputs
CPA projection to completion; sensitivity range (optimistic / pessimistic)
Tools
CPA projection model; BER subscription; Stats SA data portal
Standard
JBCC CPA provisions; BER methodology; Stats SA CPA indices
  • Work Instruction: Obtain the latest published BER Building Cost Index and Stats SA PPI indices
  • Calculate the current CPA percentage: (current index − base index) ÷ base index × 100
  • Determine the annual escalation trend from the last 6–12 months of published data
  • Project the index forward to the expected completion date using the trend rate
  • Calculate the projected CPA value at completion: remaining contract value × projected CPA %
  • Prepare a sensitivity range: optimistic (inflation decreases), base (inflation constant), pessimistic (inflation increases)
  • ✓ Quality Check: Latest indices verified against published source; trend calculation documented; projection reasonable vs economic outlook
TASK 1.7.1-05

Calculate Projected Final Cost (PFC)

Combine all PFC components to calculate the total expected project cost at completion. The PFC formula: Original Contract Sum + Approved Variations + Pending/Anticipated Variations + PS Adjustments + CPA Projection + Professional Fees − Contingency Used = PFC.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
All outputs from Tasks 1.7.1-01 to 1.7.1-04; professional fee schedules; contingency register
Outputs
PFC total; component breakdown; three-scenario analysis (best / likely / worst)
Tools
PFC calculation model; master financial register; scenario analysis template
Standard
SACPCMP Practice Note 3; projected final cost methodology
  • Work Instruction: Populate the PFC model with each component: (A) Original contract sum, (B) Approved variations, (C) Pending variations (best estimate), (D) Anticipated variations (probability-weighted), (E) PS adjustments, (F) CPA projection, (G) Professional fees remaining
  • Calculate PFC = A + B + C + D + E + F + G (note: contingency is reported separately as available / used, not included in PFC total)
  • Prepare three scenarios: Best case (only approved VOs, low CPA), Likely (base estimates), Worst case (all anticipated VOs materialize, high CPA)
  • Compare PFC against the approved project budget (which includes contingency) to determine the budget status
  • Calculate: PFC vs approved budget = variance amount and variance %
  • ✓ Quality Check: All components included; PFC reconciles to component sum; three scenarios calculated; variance computed
TASK 1.7.1-06

Prepare Budget Variance Analysis with Commentary

Produce a detailed budget variance analysis comparing the PFC against the approved budget. Provide commentary explaining each significant variance driver — which variations, cost increases, or savings are moving the PFC relative to budget.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
1 working day
Inputs
PFC from Task 1.7.1-05; approved project budget; previous month's PFC (for trend)
Outputs
Variance report with commentary; trend analysis (PFC movement month-on-month)
Tools
Variance analysis template; charting tools; PFC trend tracker
Standard
SACPCMP reporting standards; employer-specific reporting format
  • Work Instruction: Present the variance in tabular format: Budget line item | Approved Budget | Current PFC | Variance (R) | Variance (%)
  • For each significant variance (typically >2% or >R100,000), provide a written commentary explaining the cause
  • Identify the top 5 variance drivers ranked by monetary impact — these are the items to focus management attention on
  • Plot the PFC trend over time (month 1 to current month) to show whether the PFC is trending up, stable, or down
  • Compare this month's PFC movement against previous month: what changed and why
  • ✓ Quality Check: All budget lines compared; commentary for material variances; trend chart updated; top 5 drivers identified
TASK 1.7.1-07

Issue Early Warning if PFC Exceeds Budget Threshold

If the PFC exceeds the approved budget by more than the agreed threshold (typically 5%), issue a formal early warning notice to the PA and employer. The early warning must quantify the overrun, explain the drivers, and propose mitigation measures.

Role
QS → Principal Agent → Employer
SLA
Immediate (same day as PFC calculation)
Inputs
PFC variance analysis from Task 1.7.1-06; approved budget; threshold criteria
Outputs
Early warning notice (if triggered); mitigation recommendations; or confirmation within budget
Tools
Early warning template; mitigation options register
Standard
NEC4 clause 15 (Early Warning); SACPCMP cost control practice
  • Work Instruction: Check the PFC variance %: if PFC exceeds approved budget (incl. contingency) by >5%, trigger the early warning process
  • Prepare the early warning notice: state the current PFC, the approved budget, the variance (R and %), and the primary driver
  • Propose mitigation measures: scope reduction, value engineering, deferred work, alternative specifications, additional funding request
  • Issue the notice to the PA with a recommendation for action; PA forwards to employer with PA's assessment
  • If PFC is within the 5% threshold, record "Within budget — no early warning required" in the PFC report
  • Track all issued early warnings and their resolution status in the early warning register
  • ✓ Quality Check: Threshold correctly applied; early warning issued promptly if triggered; mitigations are actionable and realistic
TASK 1.7.1-08

Present PFC Report at Monthly Project Meeting

Present the monthly PFC report and budget status at the project meeting. Ensure the financial position is formally minuted, decisions on early warnings are recorded, and any budget-related actions are assigned with deadlines.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
Monthly project meeting (per programme schedule)
Inputs
PFC report; variance analysis; early warning notices; contingency status
Outputs
Meeting minutes recording financial status; action items assigned; decisions documented
Tools
PFC presentation slides; project meeting agenda; minutes template
Standard
SACPCMP meeting protocols; project-specific reporting requirements
  • Work Instruction: Prepare a concise financial summary slide/page: approved budget, current PFC, variance, contingency remaining, key risks
  • Present at the monthly project meeting — cover: (1) expenditure to date, (2) PFC status, (3) variance drivers, (4) early warnings, (5) contingency status
  • For any early warnings issued, present the mitigation options and facilitate a decision from the employer/PA
  • Ensure the meeting minutes accurately record: PFC figure, budget status (on/over/under), decisions taken, actions assigned
  • Distribute the PFC report to all project team members and the funder (if required by funding agreement)
  • ✓ Quality Check: Financial status presented and minuted; early warnings addressed; actions assigned with deadlines; report distributed

1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Valuation Calculation & Payment Certificate sub-process into granular work instructions. Each task addresses a component of the interim payment certificate: aggregating measured work values, calculating preliminaries, including approved variations and CPA adjustments, verifying materials on site, applying contractual deductions (retention, previous payments, penalties), and preparing the signed certificate for issue. The payment certificate is a contractual obligation per JBCC clause 31 requiring employer payment within 30 days of issue.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.5.2-01

Aggregate Measured Work Values by BoQ Section

Compile cumulative measured work values from the site measurement records (D1.5-SP-001 output). Multiply the QS-assessed quantity for each BoQ item by the contract rate to produce the cumulative value to date per item and per section.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
1 working day
Inputs
D1.5-SP-001 measurement records; priced BoQ with contract rates; previous valuation
Outputs
Measured work total (cumulative); section breakdown schedule
Tools
Valuation spreadsheet; platform valuation module; BoQ pricing data
Standard
JBCC PBA clause 33; ASAQS measurement conventions
  • Work Instruction: For each BoQ line item, enter the QS-assessed cumulative quantity from the measurement records
  • Multiply quantity × contract rate = cumulative value for each item (ensure rates include any agreed adjustments)
  • Sum items within each BoQ section: Substructure, Superstructure, Roof, Finishes, External Works, etc.
  • Compare this month's cumulative value against previous month to verify the movement is reasonable
  • Cross-reference high-value items against measurement notes to confirm accuracy before proceeding
  • ✓ Quality Check: Every BoQ item valued; section totals sum to measured work total; movement from previous cert is logical
TASK 1.5.2-02

Calculate Preliminaries Claim

Calculate the contractor's preliminaries entitlement for the current valuation period. Time-based preliminary items are pro-rated against contract duration, while fixed/once-off items are valued based on actual completion or delivery to site.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
Contract programme; preliminaries schedule from BoQ; contract duration; time elapsed
Outputs
Prelim value for the current period; cumulative prelim total
Tools
Preliminaries calculation template; contract programme
Standard
JBCC clause 25 (Contract Sum components); GCC 2015 prelim provisions
  • Work Instruction: Classify each prelim item as time-based (e.g., site establishment maintained monthly) or fixed (e.g., site establishment once-off)
  • For time-based items: calculate value = item amount ÷ contract duration (months) × months elapsed to date
  • For fixed items: assess completion status — 0% (not started), 50% (partially delivered), or 100% (complete)
  • If the contractor has an approved extension of time, adjust the time-based denominator accordingly
  • Compile the cumulative prelim value and add to the measured work total from Task 1.5.2-01
  • ✓ Quality Check: All prelim items classified; time-based calculations use correct duration; fixed items verified on site
TASK 1.5.2-03

Include Approved Variations from Variation Register

Extract all approved variation orders (VOs) from the D1.6 variation register and include their agreed values in the current valuation. Only include variations with formal employer approval and agreed values — pending or anticipated variations are excluded from the payment certificate.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
D1.6 variation register; approved VO documentation; previous certificate variation total
Outputs
Variation total for inclusion in payment certificate; updated VO schedule
Tools
Variation register; VO tracking spreadsheet; platform variation module
Standard
JBCC clause 29 (Adjustment of Contract Value); NEC4 clause 63
  • Work Instruction: Filter the D1.6 variation register for status = "Approved" with an agreed monetary value
  • For each approved VO: verify the employer approval letter/instruction is on file and the value is formally agreed
  • Assess the work progress for each VO — only include the proportional value of work actually completed (not the full VO if partially done)
  • Sum all approved and completed VO values to produce the cumulative variation total
  • Calculate the movement: current cumulative variation total − previous certificate variation total = variation amount this period
  • ✓ Quality Check: Only approved VOs included; employer approval on file for each; partial completion correctly assessed
TASK 1.5.2-04

Include CPA Adjustment & Verify Materials on Site

Add the cumulative Contract Price Adjustment from D1.4-SP-002 and verify the contractor's materials-on-site claim. Materials on site require physical verification (items present on site) and documentary verification (invoices proving ownership and value).

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
D1.4-SP-002 CPA calculation; contractor material invoices; site inspection of stored materials
Outputs
CPA amount included; verified materials-on-site value
Tools
CPA calculation module; materials verification checklist
Standard
JBCC CPA provisions; BER Building Cost Index; Stats SA PPI (CPA indices)
  • Work Instruction: Obtain the latest CPA calculation from D1.4-SP-002 — verify the current index values match the latest published BER / Stats SA data
  • Include the cumulative CPA amount in the valuation schedule as a separate line item
  • For materials on site: obtain the contractor's claim with supporting invoices for each material batch
  • Physically verify materials are present on site, properly stored, and match the invoiced quantities and descriptions
  • Exclude materials not yet delivered, materials already incorporated into measured work, and materials without valid invoices
  • Calculate the verified materials-on-site value and add to the cumulative valuation
  • ✓ Quality Check: CPA uses latest published indices; materials physically verified; invoices validated; no double-counting with measured work
TASK 1.5.2-05

Apply Deductions: Retention, Previous Payments & Penalties

Apply all contractual deductions to arrive at the nett amount due. Deduct retention per D1.4-SP-004, subtract cumulative previous certificates paid, and apply any penalty deductions for late completion or contractual non-compliance as instructed by the PA.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
D1.4-SP-004 retention schedule; previous certificate totals; PA penalty instructions
Outputs
Nett amount due (excl. VAT); deduction schedule
Tools
Retention calculator; previous certificate register; penalty tracker
Standard
JBCC clause 32 (Retention); NEC clause 51.3 (Option X16); GCC 2015 clause 44
  • Work Instruction: Calculate cumulative gross value = measured work + prelims + variations + CPA + materials on site
  • Deduct retention: cumulative gross × retention % (typically 5%), capped at the retention limit from D1.4-SP-004
  • If cumulative retention has reached the retention limit, no further retention is deducted on additional work
  • Deduct previous cumulative certificates paid: nett amount = cumulative gross − retention − previous certificates paid
  • Apply any PA-instructed penalties (e.g., late completion penalties per JBCC clause 24) — document the authority for each deduction
  • Produce the nett amount due before VAT, with a clear reconciliation schedule showing each deduction
  • ✓ Quality Check: Retention correctly capped at limit; previous certificates match payment records; penalty deductions have PA authority
TASK 1.5.2-06

Add VAT & Prepare Payment Certificate Document

Apply VAT (15%) to the nett amount due and prepare the formal Interim Payment Certificate (IPC) document. The certificate must include the full valuation schedule, component breakdown, deduction summary, and the final amount payable including VAT.

Role
Quantity Surveyor
SLA
0.5 working day
Inputs
Nett amount due from Task 1.5.2-05; valuation schedule; IPC template
Outputs
Draft Interim Payment Certificate with supporting schedule
Tools
IPC template; valuation schedule; document formatting tools
Standard
SARS VAT Act (15%); JBCC IPC format requirements
  • Work Instruction: Calculate VAT: nett amount due × 15% = VAT amount; total payable = nett + VAT
  • Prepare the IPC document using the standard template — populate all fields: certificate number, valuation period, contract details
  • Attach the valuation schedule showing: (A) Measured Works, (B) Prelims, (C) Variations, (D) CPA, (E) Materials on Site, (F) Less Retention, (G) Less Previous, (H) Less Penalties, (I) VAT, (J) Total Payable
  • Include a one-page executive summary with: total certified to date, amount due this period, percentage of contract sum expended
  • Review the complete document for arithmetic accuracy before routing for signature
  • ✓ Quality Check: VAT correctly applied; arithmetic verified; all components shown; template fully populated; certificate numbered sequentially
TASK 1.5.2-07

Obtain QS & PA Signatures and Issue to Employer

Route the completed Interim Payment Certificate for QS signature, then PA co-signature. Once signed by both parties, formally issue the certificate to the employer, triggering the contractual payment obligation (30 days per JBCC or as specified in contract data).

Role
QS → Principal Agent → Employer
SLA
2 working days (signature cycle)
Inputs
Draft IPC from Task 1.5.2-06; supporting valuation schedule
Outputs
Signed IPC; proof of delivery to employer; payment due date recorded
Tools
Digital signature platform; document management system; email / courier tracking
Standard
JBCC clause 31 (Payment within 30 days); NEC4 clause 51.2
  • Work Instruction: QS reviews the final IPC document one last time, then signs (physical or digital signature)
  • Route to the Principal Agent for review and co-signature — PA has 2 working days to review and sign
  • Once both signatures are obtained, issue the IPC to the employer with a formal cover letter stating the amount due and payment due date
  • Record the issue date — the employer's 30-day payment period commences from this date per JBCC clause 31
  • Distribute copies: employer (original), contractor (copy), project file (copy), funder (copy if required)
  • Update the payment certificate register with: cert number, issue date, amount, payment due date
  • ✓ Quality Check: Both signatures obtained; employer receipt confirmed; payment due date calculated and recorded; all parties notified

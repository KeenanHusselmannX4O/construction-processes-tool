1. Task Overview

This task set decomposes the Comparative Analysis & Tender Report sub-process (D1.3-SP-003) into seven granular work instructions. The tasks cover the full evaluation-to-recommendation workflow: preparing section-by-section price comparisons, analysing rate reasonableness, identifying front-loading risks, applying the PPPFA preference point formula (where applicable), compiling the formal tender evaluation report, obtaining Senior QS sign-off, and presenting the recommendation at the tender award meeting. All tasks comply with PPPFA 2017, SACPCMP Practice Notes, CIDB evaluation frameworks, and SACQSP professional guidelines.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.3.3-01

Prepare Section-by-Section Price Comparison Schedule

Description
Import corrected priced BoQs from all qualified tenderers and prepare a structured comparative schedule. For each trade section (Prelims, Preambles, Earthworks, Concrete, Masonry, etc.), tabulate each tenderer's section total side by side, calculate the mean section price, identify percentage variance from mean per tenderer per section, and highlight sections where any tenderer exceeds ±15% variance from the mean. Include a summary row showing corrected tender total, percentage of QS estimate, and rank position.
Role
QS
Inputs
  • Corrected priced BoQs (all qualified tenderers)
  • QS elemental estimate / pre-tender estimate
  • Corrected tender ranking schedule (D1.3-SP-002)
Outputs
  • Section-by-section comparative schedule
  • Variance analysis per trade per tenderer
  • Summary comparison chart
Tools / Templates
Comparative Schedule Template (Excel); QS software (CostX / QSPlus); Variance highlighting rules (conditional formatting)
SLA
2–3 working days

✔ Quality Check: All qualified tenderers included, all trade sections represented, corrected totals used (not original), variance percentages calculated correctly, QS estimate comparison included

TASK 1.3.3-02

Analyse Rate Reasonableness Across Tenderers

Description
For each significant measured item (top 80% by value — Pareto principle), compare the unit rate across all tenderers and against the QS estimate rate. Calculate the mean rate and standard deviation. Flag any rate that deviates more than ±25% from the mean as a statistical outlier. For flagged items, assess whether the rate is commercially reasonable considering: current market conditions, material cost indices, labour rates per Master Builders Association schedules, and geographical location factors. Prepare a rate analysis narrative for each flagged item explaining the risk assessment.
Role
QS
Inputs
  • Comparative schedule (TASK 1.3.3-01)
  • QS elemental estimate with unit rates
  • Current construction cost indices (AECOM / Rode / StatsSA)
  • Master Builders Association labour rate schedules
Outputs
  • Rate analysis report with outlier flags
  • Risk narrative per flagged item
  • Rate comparison matrix (item-level)
Tools / Templates
Rate Analysis Template; Construction cost index databases; Statistical outlier calculation (Excel); Historical project rate database
SLA
2–3 working days
Decision Criteria
Rate deviation > ±25% from mean = flagged for review; abnormally low rates may indicate tenderer error or intent to claim variations; abnormally high rates may indicate front-loading or specification misunderstanding

✔ Quality Check: Top 80% value items analysed, outlier threshold consistently applied, risk narratives provided for all flagged items, market data sources cited

TASK 1.3.3-03

Identify Front-Loading & Pricing Distribution Assessment

Description
Analyse the pricing distribution across project stages to identify front-loading patterns. Calculate the value-weighted percentage of the tender total allocated to early-stage (Months 1–3) vs mid-stage and late-stage activities for each tenderer. Compare distributions across all tenderers. Front-loading indicators include: disproportionately high Prelims percentage (>15% of contract value), elevated earthworks/substructure rates relative to superstructure, high Day Works provisions, and inflated early-trade pricing. Assess the financial risk of front-loading to the Employer (excess early payment exposure).
Role
Senior QS
Inputs
  • Item-level pricing data (all tenderers)
  • Indicative construction programme
  • Comparative schedule (TASK 1.3.3-01)
  • Industry benchmarks for trade percentage distributions
Outputs
  • Front-loading assessment per tenderer
  • Pricing distribution analysis (by project stage)
  • Financial risk assessment for Employer
Tools / Templates
Front-Loading Assessment Template; Value distribution chart (Excel); Industry benchmark data (ASAQS / AECOM); Cash flow projection model
SLA
1 working day
Decision Criteria
Prelims > 15% of contract value = investigate; early-stage pricing > 40% of total within first 25% of programme = potential front-loading flag; material front-loading may warrant recommendation for revised payment schedule or increased retention

✔ Quality Check: Distribution analysis completed for all tenderers, front-loading flags substantiated with quantitative evidence, risk mitigation recommendations provided where applicable

TASK 1.3.3-04

Apply PPPFA Preference Point Formula

Description
For government-funded projects where PPPFA 2017 applies, calculate preference points for each qualified tenderer. Determine the applicable formula: 80/20 for contracts ≤ R50 million or 90/10 for contracts > R50 million. Calculate price points: Ps = X(1 – (Pt – Pmin)/Pmin) where X = 80 or 90, Pt = tenderer's corrected total, Pmin = lowest corrected total. Calculate B-BBEE preference points based on verified B-BBEE level per the PPPFA Regulation Schedule. Sum price points + preference points = total score. Rank tenderers by total score (highest to lowest). For private-sector projects where PPPFA does not apply, rank on price and agreed qualitative criteria.
Role
QS
Inputs
  • Corrected tender totals (all qualified tenderers)
  • Verified B-BBEE levels per tenderer
  • PPPFA 2017 Regulation Schedules
  • Contract value threshold determination
Outputs
  • PPPFA preference point scorecard
  • Price point calculation per tenderer
  • B-BBEE preference point allocation
  • Final scored ranking (highest total score first)
Tools / Templates
PPPFA Scorecard Template; B-BBEE points allocation table (per PPPFA Schedule); Preference point calculator (Excel)
SLA
0.5 working day
Decision Criteria
80/20 applies when contract value ≤ R50m; 90/10 when > R50m; B-BBEE Level 1 = maximum preference points (20 or 10); non-compliant = 0 preference points; formula must yield non-negative price points

✔ Quality Check: Correct formula applied per contract value, B-BBEE points match verified certificates, calculations independently verified, scorecard compliant with PPPFA Regulation format

TASK 1.3.3-05

Compile Formal Tender Evaluation Report

Description
Compile the comprehensive tender evaluation report incorporating all analysis outputs. Report structure: (1) Executive Summary with recommendation; (2) Tender Process Summary (dates, tenderers invited, submissions received); (3) Qualification Results (compliance matrix summary); (4) Arithmetic Check Summary (error percentages, corrections applied); (5) Price Comparison Analysis (section-by-section with variances); (6) Rate Reasonableness Assessment (outlier flags and risk narratives); (7) Front-Loading Assessment; (8) PPPFA Scoring (if applicable); (9) Comparison to QS Estimate; (10) Award Recommendation with justification; (11) Appendices (all supporting schedules). The recommendation must clearly state the recommended tenderer, key reasons, and any conditions or caveats.
Role
QS
Inputs
  • Comparative schedule (TASK 1.3.3-01)
  • Rate analysis report (TASK 1.3.3-02)
  • Front-loading assessment (TASK 1.3.3-03)
  • PPPFA scorecard (TASK 1.3.3-04)
  • Arithmetic check summaries (D1.3-SP-002)
  • Qualification matrix (D1.3-SP-002)
Outputs
  • Draft tender evaluation report (complete)
  • Supporting appendices package
  • Award recommendation summary
Tools / Templates
Tender Evaluation Report Template; SACQSP professional report format; Award Recommendation Template
SLA
2 working days

✔ Quality Check: All 11 report sections complete, recommendation clearly stated with justification, all appendices attached, report formatted per SACQSP professional standards

TASK 1.3.3-06

Senior QS Review & Sign-Off

Description
Independent Senior QS / Director reviews the draft tender evaluation report for accuracy, completeness, and professional quality. Verify: (1) arithmetic in all schedules is correct; (2) PPPFA calculations comply with regulations; (3) rate analysis conclusions are defensible; (4) front-loading assessment is adequately substantiated; (5) recommendation is supported by the evidence presented; (6) report language is objective and appropriate for professional submission; (7) no conflicts of interest or procedural irregularities. Provide sign-off or return with markup for revision.
Role
Senior QS / Director (SACQSP registered)
Inputs
  • Draft tender evaluation report (TASK 1.3.3-05)
  • All supporting schedules and appendices
  • SACQSP Code of Professional Conduct
Outputs
  • Approved tender evaluation report (signed)
  • Review comments / markup (if revisions required)
Tools / Templates
Report Review Checklist; SACQSP professional sign-off cover sheet; Document markup tools
SLA
1–2 working days
Decision Criteria
Report must be reviewed by SACQSP-registered Professional QS; reviewer must not have been involved in the arithmetic check or comparative analysis to ensure independence; any material revisions require re-review

✔ Quality Check: Senior QS sign-off obtained, all review comments addressed and closed out, report carries SACQSP registration number of signing professional

TASK 1.3.3-07

Present to PA / Employer at Tender Award Meeting

Description
Present the approved tender evaluation report at the formal tender award meeting convened by the PA. Attendees typically include: Employer / Client representative, PA, QS, and relevant design team members. Walk through: (1) tender process overview; (2) qualification results; (3) price comparison highlights; (4) rate anomalies and risks identified; (5) front-loading concerns; (6) PPPFA scores (if applicable); (7) QS estimate comparison; (8) formal award recommendation. Answer questions from the Employer. Record the Employer's award decision and any conditions. Issue the formal letter of acceptance to the successful tenderer and notification letters to unsuccessful tenderers per the agreed process.
Role
QS (presenter) / PA (chair) / Employer (decision maker)
Inputs
  • Approved tender evaluation report (TASK 1.3.3-06)
  • Presentation summary / slides
  • Tender award meeting agenda
Outputs
  • Award decision (recorded in minutes)
  • Tender award meeting minutes (signed)
  • Letter of acceptance (to successful tenderer)
  • Notification letters (to unsuccessful tenderers)
Tools / Templates
Tender Award Meeting Agenda Template; Meeting Minutes Template; Letter of Acceptance Template; Unsuccessful Tenderer Notification Template
SLA
Meeting (typically 1–2 hours); letters issued within 2 working days of decision
Decision Criteria
Employer makes final award decision; QS recommendation is advisory; for PPPFA projects, award must go to highest-scoring tenderer unless objective grounds for exclusion exist; award letters must reference tender validity period

✔ Quality Check: Meeting minutes signed by all attendees, award decision formally recorded, acceptance letter issued within tender validity period, unsuccessful tenderer notifications dispatched

1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Final Measurement & Final Account sub-process into granular work instructions. Each task covers a critical step in achieving financial close-out of the construction contract: conducting the final site measurement of all outstanding quantities, closing open variation orders, reconciling provisional sum and prime cost adjustments, compiling the draft Final Account statement, reconciling against the contractor's submission, negotiating disputed items, issuing the agreed Final Account with the Final Payment Certificate, and releasing remaining retention. Per JBCC clause 34, the Final Account must be agreed within 6 months of Practical Completion.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.8.1-01

Conduct Final Site Measurement of All Outstanding Quantities

Perform a comprehensive final measurement of all work completed under the contract. This differs from interim measurement in that every trade section must be measured to final quantities — no estimates or provisional percentages are acceptable. Use as-built drawings, site records, and physical measurement to establish definitive quantities for the re-measurement adjustment.

Role
QS + Contractor QS
SLA
2–4 weeks (dependent on project size)
Inputs
As-built drawings; BoQ; all interim measurement records; site access; previous valuation data
Outputs
Final measurement schedules per BoQ section; re-measurement adjustment schedule
Tools
Laser distance meter; as-built drawings; measurement book / tablet; ASAQS measurement rules
Standard
JBCC clause 34; ASAQS Standard System of Measuring; NEC4 clause 53
  • Work Instruction: Schedule a joint final measurement programme with the contractor QS — agree on the sequence of trades to be measured and the timeline
  • Measure every BoQ section to final quantities using as-built drawings and physical site measurement — no provisional allowances
  • For earthworks: obtain final survey data (cut/fill volumes) from the land surveyor; reconcile against BoQ quantities
  • For concrete/structural: take final dimensions from as-built drawings; verify against site measurement for any deviations
  • For finishes and services: use unit-by-unit registers to confirm completion status of every item
  • Record all measurements with clear BoQ item references; both parties to sign each measurement sheet
  • Calculate the re-measurement adjustment: final measured quantity − original BoQ quantity = over/under measurement per item
  • ✓ Quality Check: Every BoQ section measured to final; joint measurement sheets signed by both QS parties; re-measurement schedule complete
TASK 1.8.1-02

Finalise All Variation Order Values

Close all open variation orders by agreeing final values with the contractor QS. Ensure every approved VO has a signed agreement on the final cost. Compile the final VO schedule showing the cumulative impact on the contract sum.

Role
QS + Contractor QS
SLA
2–4 weeks
Inputs
VO register (D1.6); individual VO valuations; contractor's VO claims; site records
Outputs
Final VO schedule with agreed values; signed VO agreements; cumulative VO impact on contract sum
Tools
VO register; valuation spreadsheet; platform VO module; contract rates schedule
Standard
JBCC clause 25 (Variations); GCC 2015 clause 6.9; NEC4 clause 63
  • Work Instruction: Extract all open VOs from the variation register — identify those not yet agreed at final value
  • For each open VO: review the approved scope, measure the actual work executed, and value using contract rates or agreed star rates
  • Compare QS valuation against the contractor's claim for each VO — negotiate and agree on disputed amounts
  • Record the agreed final value for each VO on the VO agreement form; both parties sign
  • Compile the final VO schedule showing: VO number, description, approved value, net impact (addition/omission), and cumulative total
  • Where agreement cannot be reached on specific VOs, record both parties' positions and note for dispute resolution
  • ✓ Quality Check: All VOs closed with signed agreements; final VO schedule complete; cumulative impact calculated; unresolved items documented
TASK 1.8.1-03

Reconcile Provisional Sum & Prime Cost Adjustments

Finalise all provisional sum (PS) and prime cost (PC) item adjustments by comparing actual expenditure against the original BoQ allowances. Calculate the net adjustment (surplus or deficit) for each PS and PC item.

Role
QS
SLA
1–2 weeks
Inputs
PS/PC registers; actual supplier invoices; BoQ PS/PC allowances; D1.4-SP-003 records
Outputs
PS/PC adjustment schedule; net adjustment per item; supporting invoice schedule
Tools
PS/PC tracking spreadsheet; invoice register; platform financial module
Standard
JBCC clause 24 (Provisional Sums); JBCC clause 22 (Prime Cost); GCC 2015
  • Work Instruction: Extract all PS and PC items from the BoQ with their original allowance values
  • For each PS item: compile all actual expenditure (invoices, sub-contractor payments, attendance) and calculate total actual cost
  • Calculate PS adjustment: actual expenditure − BoQ allowance = adjustment (addition if over, omission if under)
  • For each PC item: compile actual supplier invoices plus contractor's profit and attendance markup as per contract
  • Calculate PC adjustment: (actual invoice + attendance) − BoQ PC allowance = adjustment
  • Prepare a consolidated PS/PC adjustment schedule with supporting invoice references for each item
  • ✓ Quality Check: All PS/PC items reconciled; actual invoices verified and cross-referenced; net adjustments calculated; schedule ready for Final Account
TASK 1.8.1-04

Compile Draft Final Account Statement

Assemble all component schedules into a single draft Final Account Statement showing the reconciliation from the original contract sum to the final adjusted contract sum. This is the definitive financial record of the contract.

Role
QS
SLA
1 week
Inputs
Re-measurement schedule (Task 1.8.1-01); VO schedule (Task 1.8.1-02); PS/PC schedule (Task 1.8.1-03); CPA records; penalty / claims records
Outputs
Draft Final Account Statement; component summary; calculation of final adjusted contract sum
Tools
Final Account template; valuation spreadsheet; platform Final Account module
Standard
JBCC clause 34 (Final Account); SACQSP Final Account guidelines
  • Work Instruction: Use the Final Account template with the following structure:
    • A. Original Contract Sum (from D1.4-SP-001)
    • B. ± Re-measurement Adjustments (from Task 1.8.1-01)
    • C. + Approved Variation Orders (from Task 1.8.1-02)
    • D. ± Provisional Sum Adjustments (from Task 1.8.1-03)
    • E. ± Prime Cost Adjustments (from Task 1.8.1-03)
    • F. + Cumulative CPA to Practical Completion (from D1.4-SP-002)
    • G. − Penalties (if applicable, per PA determination)
    • H. + Agreed Claims (if any)
    • = Final Account Total
  • Cross-check each component total against the underlying schedules — ensure R0 tolerance
  • Prepare a one-page executive summary showing the original contract sum, each adjustment category, and the final total
  • Attach all supporting schedules as appendices to the draft Final Account Statement
  • ✓ Quality Check: All components included; arithmetic verified to R0 tolerance; executive summary prepared; supporting appendices attached
TASK 1.8.1-05

Reconcile Against Contractor's Final Account Submission

Compare the QS-prepared draft Final Account against the contractor's own final account submission. Prepare a detailed item-by-item reconciliation highlighting agreements and discrepancies.

Role
QS + Contractor QS
SLA
2–4 weeks
Inputs
QS Draft Final Account (Task 1.8.1-04); contractor's final account submission; supporting documentation from both parties
Outputs
Item-by-item reconciliation schedule; agreed items list; disputed items list with both parties' positions
Tools
Reconciliation template; Final Account comparison spreadsheet; platform reconciliation module
Standard
JBCC clause 34; fair dealing per SACQSP Professional Code of Conduct
  • Work Instruction: Line up the QS Final Account and contractor's submission side-by-side at the component level (A through H)
  • For each component, drill down to the individual schedule items and compare values
  • Categorise each item as: Agreed (values match within 1%), Minor Variance (1–5%), or Disputed (>5% or fundamentally different basis)
  • For disputed items: document the QS position, the contractor's position, the variance amount, and the evidence supporting each position
  • Prepare a reconciliation summary: total agreed value, total disputed value, and the range of possible final account outcomes
  • Schedule a reconciliation meeting with the contractor QS to work through disputed items systematically
  • ✓ Quality Check: Every line item compared; categorisation complete; disputed items documented with both positions; reconciliation meeting scheduled
TASK 1.8.1-06

Negotiate & Agree Disputed Items

Conduct structured negotiations with the contractor QS to resolve all disputed items section by section. Apply evidence-based negotiation using measurement records, contract provisions, and precedent. Aim for agreement within the JBCC 6-month window from Practical Completion.

Role
QS + Contractor QS (escalate to PA if required)
SLA
4–8 weeks
Inputs
Reconciliation schedule (Task 1.8.1-05); evidence packs per disputed item; contract conditions
Outputs
Negotiation records; agreed settlement per item; escalation records for unresolved items
Tools
Negotiation tracker; evidence pack templates; contract reference documents
Standard
JBCC clause 34 (6-month deadline); JBCC clause 40 (dispute resolution); NEC4 clause 53
  • Work Instruction: Prepare an evidence pack for each disputed item containing: measurement records, photographs, contract clause references, and the QS rationale
  • Schedule negotiation meetings — work through items in order of value (highest-value disputes first)
  • For each item: present both positions, review supporting evidence, identify the contractual basis for the correct interpretation
  • Record the agreed settlement amount for each item — both parties sign the negotiation record
  • For items where agreement cannot be reached: formally record both positions and escalate to the Principal Agent per JBCC clause 40
  • If PA determination does not resolve, initiate contractual dispute resolution (mediation / adjudication / arbitration per contract)
  • Track the JBCC 6-month deadline — escalate proactively if resolution is not progressing
  • ✓ Quality Check: All disputed items addressed; negotiation records signed; unresolved items formally escalated; 6-month deadline tracked
TASK 1.8.1-07

Issue Agreed Final Account & Final Payment Certificate

Formalise the agreed Final Account by issuing the Final Account Statement for contractor signature. Prepare and issue the Final Payment Certificate reflecting the final balance due (or owed) after deducting all previous payments.

Role
QS → PA for certification
SLA
1 week
Inputs
Agreed Final Account; cumulative payment certificate record; retention balance
Outputs
Signed Final Account Statement; Final Payment Certificate; payment instruction to Employer
Tools
Final Account template; payment certificate template; platform certificate module
Standard
JBCC clause 34 (Final Account); clause 33 (payment certificate); SACQSP
  • Work Instruction: Update the Final Account Statement with all agreed values from the negotiation phase (Task 1.8.1-06)
  • Calculate the Final Payment Certificate: Final Account Total − cumulative payments to date (all previous certificates) = final balance due
  • If balance is positive: amount due to contractor; if negative: amount owed by contractor to employer
  • Submit the Final Account Statement to the contractor for signature — allow 7 days for review and signature
  • Submit the Final Payment Certificate to the PA for certification and issue to the Employer
  • Instruct the Employer on the final payment amount and due date per JBCC payment terms
  • ✓ Quality Check: Final Account signed by both parties; Final Payment Certificate issued; payment instruction to Employer; amounts reconcile to R0
TASK 1.8.1-08

Release Remaining Retention & Prepare Financial Close-out Report

Release any remaining retention balance held under the contract and prepare the financial close-out report summarising the complete financial history of the project from award to final account.

Role
QS
SLA
1 week
Inputs
Final Payment Certificate (Task 1.8.1-07); retention register; defects completion certificate; all financial records
Outputs
Retention release instruction; financial close-out report; archived contract financial records
Tools
Retention register; close-out report template; document management system
Standard
JBCC clause 26 (retention); SACQSP record-keeping; project audit requirements
  • Work Instruction: Confirm the defects completion certificate has been issued by the PA (coordinate with D1.8-SP-002 Defects Liability)
  • Calculate the remaining retention balance after any defect remedy deductions
  • Prepare the retention release instruction to the Employer for the remaining retention amount
  • Compile the financial close-out report covering: original contract sum, all adjustments, final account total, total payments made, retention released, and any outstanding matters
  • Include a lessons-learned section highlighting key financial outcomes: actual vs budget, major VO impacts, and claims history
  • Archive all contract financial records in the project document management system per SACQSP record-keeping requirements (minimum 5 years)
  • ✓ Quality Check: Retention fully released; close-out report complete; all records archived; financial obligations discharged; contract financially closed

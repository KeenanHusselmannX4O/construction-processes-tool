1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Defects Liability Financial Close-out sub-process into granular work instructions covering the full defects liability period (DLP) from Practical Completion to Final Completion. Tasks include: releasing the first moiety of retention, establishing and maintaining the defects tracking register, costing unrectified defects for third-party remedy, coordinating NHBRC inspection clearance (for affordable housing), reconciling retention deductions, releasing the second moiety upon DLP expiry, and issuing the Final Completion Certificate to achieve contractual close-out. Per JBCC clause 26, the DLP is typically 3–12 months.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.8.2-01

Release First Moiety of Retention upon Practical Completion

Upon issue of the Practical Completion certificate by the Principal Agent, prepare and issue the instruction to release the first moiety (50%) of the retention held under the contract. Verify the retention balance against the retention register and payment certificate records.

Role
QS → PA for certification
SLA
Within 7 days of Practical Completion
Inputs
Practical Completion certificate; retention register; cumulative payment certificates
Outputs
First moiety release instruction; updated retention register; payment certificate addendum
Tools
Retention register; payment certificate template; platform financial module
Standard
JBCC clause 26.1; retention rate per contract (typically 5–10% capped at specified amount)
  • Work Instruction: Confirm receipt of the Practical Completion certificate from the PA — verify the date of Practical Completion
  • Calculate the total retention held: sum of all monthly retention deductions from payment certificates to date
  • Calculate the first moiety: 50% of total retention held = amount to be released
  • Verify that the retention percentage and cap are correct per the contract conditions (JBCC typically 5% capped at a specified R amount)
  • Prepare the first moiety release instruction addressed to the Employer — include the retention calculation, amount to release, and contractor banking details
  • Update the retention register: record first moiety released; calculate remaining retention balance (second moiety)
  • ✓ Quality Check: Retention calculation verified against payment certificates; release instruction issued within 7 days; register updated; PA notified
TASK 1.8.2-02

Establish & Maintain Defects Tracking Register

Set up a defects tracking register from the PA's defects list issued at Practical Completion. Monitor and record the contractor's rectification progress throughout the DLP. Generate status reports for the project team and track outstanding items against DLP expiry.

Role
QS + PA
SLA
Register setup within 5 days of Practical Completion; ongoing updates throughout DLP
Inputs
PA's defects list; Practical Completion certificate; contract DLP duration; NHBRC inspection requirements
Outputs
Defects tracking register; monthly status reports; rectification progress dashboard
Tools
Defects register template; project management system; platform defects module
Standard
JBCC clause 26 (Defects Liability); NHBRC Act (for housing projects)
  • Work Instruction: Obtain the PA's defects list — enter each defect item into the tracking register with: item number, description, location, severity, and photo reference
  • Set up the DLP timeline: record Practical Completion date, DLP duration per contract, and calculated DLP expiry date
  • Issue the defects list to the contractor with a formal instruction to rectify within the DLP period
  • Conduct monthly site inspections to record contractor rectification progress — update the register status per item: Outstanding / In Progress / Rectified / Disputed
  • For NHBRC-enrolled housing projects: coordinate the NHBRC defects inspection schedule and record NHBRC clearance status per unit/section
  • Generate monthly defects status reports for the PA and project team — highlight overdue items and approaching DLP expiry
  • ✓ Quality Check: All defects from PA list captured; regular updates maintained; NHBRC coordination active; DLP expiry date prominently tracked
TASK 1.8.2-03

Cost Unrectified Defects for Third-Party Remedy

Where the contractor fails to rectify defects within the DLP period or refuses to remedy specific items, the QS must cost the outstanding works for third-party remedy. These costs are deducted from the retention before the second moiety release.

Role
QS
SLA
1–2 weeks (before DLP expiry)
Inputs
Outstanding defects list; defects register status; market rates for remedy works; contractor correspondence
Outputs
Defect remedy cost schedule; third-party remedy quotations; deduction calculation for retention
Tools
Cost estimation templates; market rate database; platform valuation module
Standard
JBCC clause 26.4 (employer's right to remedy); GCC 2015; common law
  • Work Instruction: Extract all defects items with status "Outstanding" or "Disputed" from the defects register as DLP expiry approaches
  • Issue a formal notice to the contractor listing outstanding defects and stating the employer's intention to arrange third-party remedy per JBCC clause 26.4
  • Prepare detailed cost estimates for each outstanding defect item — include materials, labour, access, and contractor overhead/profit markup
  • Where possible, obtain competitive quotations from qualified contractors for the remedy works
  • Compile the defect remedy cost schedule showing: defect item, description, estimated cost, quotation reference, and total deduction
  • Calculate the total deduction from retention: sum of all remedy costs ≤ remaining retention balance (second moiety)
  • ✓ Quality Check: All outstanding defects costed; contractor formally notified; quotations obtained where practical; deduction within retention balance
TASK 1.8.2-04

Coordinate NHBRC Inspection Clearance (Affordable Housing)

For government-funded and NHBRC-enrolled housing projects, coordinate the NHBRC final inspection and obtain the NHBRC clearance certificate (sometimes called "Happy Letter"). This is a prerequisite for second moiety release on subsidised housing projects and for the occupation certificate.

Role
QS + PM + PA
SLA
2–4 weeks (NHBRC processing time varies by province)
Inputs
NHBRC enrolment number; defects rectification evidence; NHBRC inspection schedule; unit completion records
Outputs
NHBRC inspection report; NHBRC clearance certificate ("Happy Letter"); NHBRC warranty enrolment confirmation
Tools
NHBRC online portal; inspection coordination schedule; platform compliance module
Standard
NHBRC Act 95 of 1998; Housing Consumer Protection Measures Act; National Housing Code
  • Work Instruction: Confirm all defects identified by NHBRC during construction stages have been rectified — cross-reference against NHBRC inspection reports
  • Request the NHBRC final inspection via the NHBRC online portal — ensure the project enrolment number is active and fees are current
  • Prepare the site for NHBRC inspection: ensure all units are complete, accessible, and defect-free
  • Attend the NHBRC inspection with the contractor; record any additional defects identified by the NHBRC inspector
  • If additional defects found: instruct the contractor to rectify and re-request NHBRC inspection
  • Upon clearance: obtain the NHBRC "Happy Letter" and file with the project compliance records
  • Note: NHBRC clearance is required before the occupation certificate can be issued by the municipality
  • ✓ Quality Check: NHBRC final inspection passed; Happy Letter obtained; filed in compliance records; occupation certificate process can proceed
TASK 1.8.2-05

Reconcile Retention After Defect Deductions

Calculate the net retention balance available for second moiety release after deducting any third-party defect remedy costs. Prepare a detailed reconciliation showing the original retention, first moiety released, defect deductions, and the net second moiety payable.

Role
QS
SLA
3 working days
Inputs
Retention register; first moiety release record; defect remedy cost schedule (Task 1.8.2-03); DLP status report
Outputs
Retention reconciliation schedule; net second moiety calculation; deduction summary for contractor
Tools
Retention register; reconciliation template; platform financial module
Standard
JBCC clause 26.5; contract retention provisions
  • Work Instruction: Start with the total retention held under the contract (from retention register)
  • Deduct the first moiety already released (Task 1.8.2-01) = remaining retention balance (second moiety gross)
  • Deduct any defect remedy costs to be withheld (Task 1.8.2-03) = net second moiety payable to contractor
  • Prepare the retention reconciliation: Total Retention − First Moiety − Defect Deductions = Net Second Moiety
  • Issue the deduction summary to the contractor with detailed breakdown and supporting evidence for each deduction
  • Allow the contractor 14 days to dispute any deductions before finalising the release
  • ✓ Quality Check: Reconciliation arithmetic verified; deductions supported by evidence; contractor notified; dispute period allowed
TASK 1.8.2-06

Release Second Moiety of Retention upon DLP Expiry

Upon expiry of the defects liability period and confirmation that all conditions are met (defects remedied or costed, NHBRC clearance obtained), prepare and issue the instruction to release the second moiety of retention to the contractor.

Role
QS → PA for certification
SLA
Within 7 days of DLP expiry
Inputs
DLP expiry confirmation; Defects Completion Certificate from PA; retention reconciliation (Task 1.8.2-05); NHBRC clearance (if applicable)
Outputs
Second moiety release instruction; Defects Completion Certificate acknowledgement; updated retention register
Tools
Retention register; release instruction template; platform financial module
Standard
JBCC clause 26.5 (second moiety); clause 34.7; NEC4 clause 45
  • Work Instruction: Confirm the DLP has expired — verify date against Practical Completion date plus contractual DLP duration
  • Confirm the PA has issued the Defects Completion Certificate — this triggers second moiety release
  • For NHBRC projects: confirm NHBRC clearance ("Happy Letter") has been obtained (Task 1.8.2-04)
  • Use the net second moiety amount from the retention reconciliation (Task 1.8.2-05)
  • Prepare the second moiety release instruction addressed to the Employer — include the net amount, contractor banking details, and supporting reconciliation
  • Update the retention register: record the second moiety release; confirm retention balance is now R0
  • ✓ Quality Check: DLP expiry confirmed; Defects Completion Certificate received; NHBRC clearance (if applicable); release instruction issued; retention register at R0
TASK 1.8.2-07

Issue Final Completion Certificate & Archive Contract Documents

Reconcile all outstanding financial matters, support the PA in issuing the Final Completion Certificate, and archive the complete set of contract financial documents. This task achieves definitive contractual and financial close-out.

Role
QS → PA
SLA
2 weeks
Inputs
Final Account (D1.8-SP-001); retention release records; all financial records; defects register (closed); NHBRC clearance
Outputs
Final Completion Certificate; complete contract archive pack; lessons-learned financial summary
Tools
Close-out checklist; document management system; archive file structure template
Standard
JBCC clause 26.7 (Final Completion); SACQSP record-keeping (5-year minimum); project audit requirements
  • Work Instruction: Confirm all financial obligations have been discharged: Final Account agreed, Final Payment Certificate issued, all retention released
  • Confirm all contractual obligations are complete: defects remedied, NHBRC clearance obtained, occupation certificate issued
  • Prepare the QS confirmation letter to the PA supporting the issue of the Final Completion Certificate
  • Compile the contract archive pack: contract documents, all payment certificates, Final Account, VO register, correspondence, defects register, close-out report
  • Prepare a lessons-learned financial summary: actual vs budget performance, major cost drivers, claims outcomes, and recommendations for future projects
  • Archive the complete pack in the document management system with appropriate indexing and access controls — minimum 5-year retention per SACQSP
  • ✓ Quality Check: All obligations discharged; Final Completion Certificate issued; archive complete and indexed; lessons learned documented; contract closed

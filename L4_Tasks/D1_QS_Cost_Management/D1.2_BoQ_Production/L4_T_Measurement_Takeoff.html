1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Measurement & Take-off sub-process (D1.2-SP-001) into granular work instructions. It covers the full cycle from receiving finalised construction drawings through to signed-off dimension sheets, following the ASAQS Standard System of Measuring Building Work (7th Edition). Each task provides step-level detail for QS practitioners and cost technicians working in the South African construction context.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.2.1-01

Receive & Register Finalised Construction Drawings

Log all incoming drawings into the drawing register. Record drawing number, revision, date received, and originating consultant. Confirm receipt with the design team in writing and file drawings in the project document management system.

Role
QS / Cost Technician
SLA
0.5 day from receipt
Inputs
Finalised architectural, structural, civil, mechanical & electrical drawings (PDF / DWG / BIM)
Outputs
Updated drawing register; receipt confirmation email
Tools / Templates
Drawing Register Template (Excel); Document Management System; email
Decision Criteria
All drawings must carry a revision number and architect/engineer stamp before acceptance

✓ Quality Check: Drawing register entries match transmittal schedule; no unsigned drawings accepted

TASK 1.2.1-02

Review Drawing Completeness Against Trade Checklist

Compare received drawings against the standard trade checklist to confirm all required trades are represented. Check for: architectural plans/sections/elevations, structural layout & details, civil site works, mechanical services, electrical reticulation, and landscape/external works. Flag any missing or incomplete sets.

Role
QS
SLA
1 day
Inputs
Drawing register; Trade completeness checklist; project scope brief
Outputs
Completed checklist with gaps identified; RFI list (if drawings missing)
Tools / Templates
Trade Drawing Checklist Template; RFI Log Template
Decision Criteria
If >2 trade drawing sets are missing → escalate to Principal Agent before proceeding

✓ Quality Check: Every ASAQS trade section has at least one corresponding drawing set; checklist signed by QS

TASK 1.2.1-03

Request Missing Drawings & Resolve RFIs

Issue formal Requests for Information (RFIs) to the relevant design consultants for missing or unclear drawings. Track RFI status in the project log. Follow up within 48 hours if no response. Do not commence measurement for affected trades until drawings are received and verified.

Role
QS
SLA
1 day to issue RFIs; 3–5 days for resolution
Inputs
Gap list from TASK 1.2.1-02; consultant contact details
Outputs
Issued RFIs; updated drawing register upon receipt of outstanding drawings
Tools / Templates
RFI Template (project standard); RFI Tracking Log
Decision Criteria
If RFIs remain unresolved after 5 working days → escalate to Principal Agent with programme impact note

✓ Quality Check: All RFIs closed or escalated before measurement commences; no measurement on unconfirmed drawings

TASK 1.2.1-04

Plan Measurement Sequence by Trade

Establish the order in which trades will be measured, following the ASAQS Standard System structure: Earthworks → Concrete & Formwork → Masonry → Waterproofing → Roof Covering → Carpentry & Joinery → Metalwork → Plastering → Tiling → Glazing → Painting → Plumbing → Electrical → External Works. Allocate trade sections to QS team members based on expertise and workload. Set internal deadlines per trade.

Role
Senior QS / QS
SLA
0.5 day
Inputs
Complete drawing set; team resource plan; project programme deadlines
Outputs
Measurement work plan with trade allocation and internal deadlines
Tools / Templates
Measurement Work Plan Template (Excel/MS Project); ASAQS trade sequence reference
Decision Criteria
Critical-path trades (structure, services) prioritised; minimum 2 days buffer before tender submission date

✓ Quality Check: Work plan reviewed by Senior QS; all trades assigned; deadlines align with project programme

TASK 1.2.1-05

Measure Building Works per ASAQS Standard

Execute measurement of all building trade sections from architectural and structural drawings. For each trade: extract dimensions from plans/sections/details, apply ASAQS measurement rules for units and deductions, and record on dimension sheets. Key rules include: masonry — deduct openings >0.5 m²; concrete — classify by mix design and element type; formwork — measured separately to concrete face area; plastering — internal and external measured separately.

Role
QS / Cost Technician
SLA
5–10 days (dependent on project scale)
Inputs
Architectural & structural drawings; ASAQS Standard System 7th Ed; project specifications
Outputs
Completed dimension sheets for building trades (Earthworks through Painting)
Tools / Templates
Dimension Sheet Template; CostX / WinQS / Excel take-off workbook; scale ruler; digital planimeter
Decision Criteria
Where drawings are ambiguous → note assumptions on dimension sheet and raise RFI; do not guess dimensions

✓ Quality Check: Each dimension sheet references drawing number and revision; all assumptions documented; arithmetic verified

TASK 1.2.1-06

Measure Civil & External Works

Measure all civil and external work items from civil engineering and landscape drawings. Includes: bulk earthworks (classify by depth ranges per ASAQS), roads and paving (m²), stormwater drainage (pipe runs by diameter, manholes enumerated), sewer reticulation, boundary walls and fencing, landscaping, and external lighting. Apply civil engineering measurement conventions for trenching and backfill.

Role
QS / Cost Technician
SLA
2–4 days
Inputs
Civil site plan; stormwater layout; geotech report; landscape plan
Outputs
Dimension sheets for External Works section
Tools / Templates
Dimension Sheet Template; CostX / Excel; earthworks volume calculator
Decision Criteria
If geotech report indicates rock → measure rock excavation separately; if no geotech → include provisional allowance and note

✓ Quality Check: Earthwork volumes cross-checked against site plan levels; drainage lengths verified against layout drawings

TASK 1.2.1-07

Measure Mechanical & Electrical Services

Measure services installations from engineer drawings. Plumbing: enumerate sanitary fittings, measure pipework by diameter and type (copper, PVC, HDPE). Electrical: enumerate light points, socket outlets, DB boards; measure cabling by type and length. HVAC: enumerate units, measure ductwork by size. Lift installations measured as prime cost items where applicable. Coordinate with services engineers for specification clarity.

Role
QS (Services Specialist where available)
SLA
2–4 days
Inputs
Mechanical drawings; electrical reticulation drawings; HVAC layout; fire services drawings
Outputs
Dimension sheets for Plumbing, Electrical, and Mechanical sections
Tools / Templates
Dimension Sheet Template; services measurement checklist; engineer specification schedules
Decision Criteria
Specialist sub-contractor items (lifts, fire suppression, generator) → include as Provisional Sums with estimated values from engineer

✓ Quality Check: Sanitary fitting count matches room schedule; pipe lengths reconcile to layout; cabling allowances verified with engineer

TASK 1.2.1-08

Cross-Check Quantities Against Drawings

Perform systematic cross-validation of measured quantities across related trades. Verify: total wall area (masonry) aligns with plaster areas; concrete volumes match formwork face areas; roof covering area is consistent with roof plan dimensions; pipe quantities correlate with fitting counts. Use spot-check method on minimum 20% of items. Document all discrepancies and resolve before finalising.

Role
QS
SLA
1–2 days
Inputs
All completed dimension sheets; original drawings
Outputs
Cross-check reconciliation report; list of corrections applied
Tools / Templates
Cross-Check Reconciliation Template; calculator / spreadsheet
Decision Criteria
Variance >5% between related quantities → investigate and correct; variance >10% → re-measure from scratch

✓ Quality Check: Cross-check report completed and signed; all variances >5% investigated; corrections initialled on dimension sheets

TASK 1.2.1-09

Compile Final Dimension Sheets & Quantity Schedules

Assemble all corrected dimension sheets into a bound set, ordered by ASAQS trade sequence. Produce clean quantity schedules summarising totals per item, ready for transfer into the Bill of Quantities. Include a measurement summary cover page listing all trades measured, measurer name, date, and drawing references used.

Role
QS / Cost Technician
SLA
1 day
Inputs
Corrected dimension sheets; cross-check report
Outputs
Bound dimension sheet set; quantity schedule summaries per trade
Tools / Templates
Quantity Schedule Template (Excel); binding / PDF compilation
Decision Criteria
Only corrected and cross-checked sheets included; no draft or unverified sheets in final set

✓ Quality Check: Cover page complete; all sheets referenced to drawings; quantity totals reconcile to individual dimension sheets

TASK 1.2.1-10

Peer Review & Senior QS Sign-off

Senior QS conducts independent review of the complete dimension sheet set and quantity schedules. Review focuses on: correct application of ASAQS measurement rules, reasonableness of quantities against project scale, completeness across all trades, and accuracy of arithmetic. Reviewer annotates corrections in red; measurer corrects and re-submits. Process repeats until sign-off is achieved.

Role
Senior QS (Reviewer); QS (Corrections)
SLA
2–3 days
Inputs
Final dimension sheet set; quantity schedules; cross-check report; ASAQS Standard System reference
Outputs
Signed-off dimension sheets ready for BoQ compilation (D1.2-SP-002)
Tools / Templates
Peer Review Checklist; SACQSP professional review guidelines
Decision Criteria
Sign-off granted when: zero unresolved review comments; all corrections verified; ASAQS compliance confirmed

✓ Quality Check: Senior QS signature on cover page; peer review checklist 100% complete; dimension sheets released to BoQ Compilation

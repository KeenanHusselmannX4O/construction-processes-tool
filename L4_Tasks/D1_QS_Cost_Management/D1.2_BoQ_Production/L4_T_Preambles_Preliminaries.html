1. Task Overview

This task set expands the Preambles & Preliminaries Drafting sub-process (D1.2-SP-003) into granular work instructions. Preliminaries define the contractor's non-measurable obligations (site establishment, management, insurance, H&S), while Preambles set trade-specific measurement rules and material specifications. These sections are critical pricing components under JBCC Series 2000, NEC4, or GCC 2015 contracts and must comply with SA regulatory requirements including the OHS Act, Construction Regulations 2014, NHBRC enrolment (housing), and SANS 10400.

2. Detailed Task Breakdown

TASK 1.2.3-01

Review Project Scope, Contract Form & Employer's Agent Agreements

Review all project inception documents to establish the framework for Preliminaries and Preambles. Confirm: contract form selected (JBCC PBA / NEC4 ECC / GCC 2015), employer's agent appointments (Principal Agent, Principal Consultant), project scope and description, site location and access conditions, contract period and key programme milestones, and any special employer requirements (e.g., NHBRC enrolment for housing projects, subsidy compliance for government-funded work). Produce a scope checklist that will drive the content of both Preliminaries and Preambles sections.

TASK 1.2.3-02

Draft General Preliminaries from Standard Template

Using the confirmed contract form, populate the General Preliminaries from the firm's standard template. JBCC Preliminaries structure includes: Employer's Requirements, Management & Administration, Contractor's Obligations, Site Facilities, Temporary Works, and Insurances & Guarantees. For NEC4: Works Information sections. For GCC 2015: Particular Conditions. Key clauses to address:

  • Site description and access (include physical address, boundaries, access routes)
  • Contract period and programme submission requirements
  • Site establishment: offices, stores, ablution, fencing, signage, temporary services (water, electricity)
  • Insurance: Contractor's All Risk (CAR) policy, public liability minimum R 10 million, professional indemnity where applicable
  • Performance guarantee: typically 10% of contract value (JBCC) or as specified
  • Retention: 5% to practical completion, 2.5% to final completion (JBCC standard)
  • Payment terms: monthly valuations per contract form provisions

TASK 1.2.3-03

Draft Project-Specific Preliminaries

Add project-specific clauses that are not covered by the standard template. These are driven by unique site conditions, employer requirements, and regulatory context. Typical project-specific items include:

  • Geotechnical conditions: reference geotech report; special founding requirements; dewatering obligations
  • Existing services and infrastructure: protection of existing buildings, roads, services during construction
  • Environmental requirements: Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) conditions, waste management plan
  • Community liaison: local labour content requirements (commonly 30% for government projects), community liaison officer
  • NHBRC enrolment and inspection requirements (for all housing projects per Housing Consumers Protection Measures Act)
  • Subsidy compliance reporting (for government-funded affordable housing)
  • Site-specific H&S requirements beyond Construction Regulations 2014 baseline

TASK 1.2.3-04

Review Preliminaries with Principal Agent & Adjust Scope Allocation

Submit the combined General and Project-Specific Preliminaries draft to the Principal Agent (PA) / Principal Consultant for review. Meeting agenda: confirm risk allocation between employer and contractor, verify scope boundaries (what is in Preliminaries vs what is in Measured Works), agree on insurance and guarantee levels, and confirm programme and payment provisions. Incorporate PA feedback and issue revised draft. Where PA and QS disagree on risk allocation, record the disagreement and escalate to employer for decision.

TASK 1.2.3-05

Draft Trade Preambles per ASAQS Standard & Model Preambles

Draft the Preambles section for each trade using the ASAQS Model Preambles for Trades as the baseline. Preambles define: measurement conventions for each trade (referencing ASAQS Standard System 7th Edition), material quality and standards (SANS references), workmanship standards, and what is deemed included in item rates. For each trade section measured in D1.2-SP-001, draft corresponding preambles covering:

  • Earthworks: classification of materials (soil types, rock definition per geotech), compaction standards
  • Concrete: mix designs per structural engineer, curing requirements, testing regime (cube tests per SANS 5863)
  • Masonry: brick/block types and classes, mortar strength per SANS 10164, bond patterns
  • Waterproofing: membrane types, guarantee requirements (minimum 10-year per ASAQS convention)
  • Roof Covering: material specifications, fixing details, minimum pitches
  • Plumbing: pipe materials per SANS standards, sanitary ware quality grades
  • Electrical: cable types per SANS 10142, DB specifications, circuit descriptions

TASK 1.2.3-06

Incorporate Design Team Specifications into Preamble Clauses

Cross-reference the architect's and engineers' specification documents against the trade preambles to ensure alignment. For each trade: verify that material specifications in preambles match the design specification exactly (e.g., concrete mix design must match structural engineer's spec, not generic ASAQS default). Where design specifications are more onerous than ASAQS standard → amend preamble to reflect. Where design specifications are silent → retain ASAQS model preamble wording and note. Coordinate with design team to resolve any ambiguities or conflicts between trades.

TASK 1.2.3-07

Quality Review Against Contract Form Requirements & SA Regulations

Senior QS performs final quality review of the complete Preliminaries and Preambles package. Review against: (1) contract form mandatory provisions — ensure all JBCC/NEC4/GCC 2015 required clauses are addressed, (2) SA regulatory compliance — OHS Act and Construction Regulations 2014 (H&S plan clause, H&S officer threshold R 130 million), NHBRC requirements (housing), BBBEE compliance provisions, CIDB contractor grading requirements, (3) internal firm QA standards — consistent formatting, correct clause numbering, no conflicting provisions. Sign off the package for insertion into the BoQ document.
